Support

Troubleshooting Guide

Quick fixes for the most common issues you might encounter.

Bot is not moving

Check if the bot is powered on and connected to WiFi. Restart the bot and reconnect. Ensure motor connections are properly wired and the battery is charged.

Bot moves in the wrong direction

Verify that the arrow keys are mapped correctly in the code. Check motor connections to ensure they are properly wired to the correct ports.

Delayed response when controlling the bot

Ensure a stable WiFi connection with good signal strength. Restart the router if needed and reduce network load. Move closer to the hotspot.

Bot does not connect to WiFi

Double-check the WiFi credentials — SSID: CGAIBOT, Password: CG@1234. Make sure the WiFi network is active and not restricted.

Traffic sign or flash card not being detected

Verify the camera is working and properly positioned. Ensure good lighting and a clear background. Retrain the Teachable Machine model with more diverse images at different angles and distances.

Slow or incorrect AI recognition

Optimize the model by reducing computational overhead. Adjust contrast, brightness, and preprocessing filters. Test under different lighting conditions and retrain if needed.

First-time AI project takes too long to run

This is normal — the first run can take up to 1 minute as the system initializes all AI modules. Subsequent runs will be much faster.
